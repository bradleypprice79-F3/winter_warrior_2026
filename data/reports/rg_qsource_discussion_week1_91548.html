Discussion Guide: Q1 – “Get Right”

Theme: “A man must Get Right before he can do anything else.” — F3 Nation

Opening (5 minutes)

  • Welcome everyone and share the purpose: to engage with Q1 “Get Right,” exploring how we align ourselves personally so we can lead and serve others.
  • Quick icebreaker: Have each guy share one word that describes how he’s feeling right now about his own Fitness, Fellowship, or Faith (just one word).
  • Read the key paragraph together (either the rowing boat analogy or summary) to set the tone:
“A man must Get Right before he can do anything else.”

Get Right – What is “Right”? (10 minutes)

The chapter defines RIGHT as “the discipline and desire to consistently promote the cause of the below over his own cause.”

  • (Fitness) His own body
  • (Fellowship) His loved ones
  • (Faith) His Creator
```

Discussion Prompt:
What does “Right” look like in each of the following—Fitness, Fellowship, and Faith? One way of describing what something is is to describe what it is not. Feel free to use that method as well.

  • (Fitness) His own body
  • (Fellowship) His loved ones
  • (Faith) His Creator
```

Reflection / Personal Check-In (10 minutes)

  • Take 2 minutes individually and jot down two things:
    1. Where I’m doing okay in Fitness / Fellowship / Faith
    2. One area where I’m not “Right” yet (e.g., lack of self-control, weak relationships, neglect of faith)
  • After the silent time, go around and ask 2–3 willing guys to share highlights—nothing too deep unless they choose to.

Discussion Questions (10 minutes)

```

Q1:

“An un-fit man is a slave to his lack of self-control.” — F3 Nation

What does that mean to you? In what ways might a lack of control in one area undermine your effectiveness in others (work, home, church)?

  • Follow-up: What is one habit you currently have (or struggle with) that reflects a lack of self-control?

Q2:

“To be able to help others, a man must first help himself.” — F3 Nation

How does this principle apply in your role (as husband, father, leader) and in your involvement with our group?

  • Follow-up: If you ignore your own “boat” (Fitness, Fellowship, Faith), who suffers? (Think of your “concentrica” — your family, your team, etc.)
```

Additional Questions (if time allows)

  • Is it possible to be so focused on being fit that one is not “Right”?
  • For example, can someone be so focused on RDG points that they miss the opportunity to Plant, Grow, Serve, and Invigorate male community leadership?

Last 5 Minutes – Action & Accountability

Q4:

Thinking practically, what is one concrete step you will take this week (or over the next month) to “Get Right” in one of the three areas (Fitness, Fellowship, Faith)?

  • Ask each guy to commit to one action.
  • Pair up with the guy or guys next to you and message them on Slack with your action.
    • If you don’t have one yet, message them that you don’t have one yet.
    • Hold each other accountable to the action—or to first coming up with one.
    • If you’re uncomfortable sharing, be as vague as needed. “I’m going to do the thing” is fine if that’s all you’ve got.
    • It’s not ideal, but if that’s where you’re at, that’s okay.
    • You know what it is—and they can encourage you to “do the thing.”

End with gratitude and encouragement.
Remind everyone: Getting Right is the foundation for living purposefully and leading well. Start with yourself—then go impact others.
