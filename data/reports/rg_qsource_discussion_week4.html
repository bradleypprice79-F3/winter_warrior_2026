Lead Right — Week 4

Get Right → Live Right → Lead Right — living right, at scale: Influencing Movement to Advantage.

Note: At the end of today you will be asked to identify a takeaway from this week. During the discussion, be mindful about what this might be for you. Start a text to yourself and jot down anything that sticks out to you.

An Effective Leader

To be an Effective leader, a man must competently apply these leadership skills:

  • Vision
  • Articulation
  • Persuasion
  • Exhortation

(These skills are explained in the subsequent chapters.)

Discussion prompt

Consider examples where you have been led effectively, even if the leader wasn’t exactly virtuous. What made that leadership effective?

There Is No Static Plateau

Either you are moving forward toward advantage or sliding backward to disadvantage. There is no point where you’ve “arrived” and no more effort is required.

Do you agree there’s no Static Plateau in life, where no effort is required?

“Maintaining Relational Rhythm is like the circus act of spinning plates on poles. The HIM knows that just as some plates need more attention than others to stay upright, some people in his Concentrica will need more care than others to stay Right.”

The plate metaphor applies beyond relationships — to fitness, work, faith, and more. Intentional focus and effort are always required to respond to life’s ever-changing situations.

Questions to discuss
  • Where in your life have you learned the hard way that balance isn’t something you “achieve” — but something you constantly adjust?
  • Which “plate” in your life tends to wobble first?
  • How do you know when it’s happening?

A Virtuous Leader

An effective leader is measured by what he does; virtue is who a leader is.

A Virtuous leader exemplifies these leadership virtues:

  • Candor
  • Commitment
  • Consistent
  • Contentment
  • Courage
Questions to discuss
  • Who in your life would you consider virtuous, even if not a great leader?
  • Which of these virtues is most natural to you, and which takes the most intentional effort?

Effectiveness + Virtue

A great leader is BOTH effective and virtuous.

“Effectiveness concerns actions performed, while Virtue is about character possessed.”

Final reflection
  • Which area do you find more challenging: being effective or being virtuous?
  • What is your takeaway from this week?
