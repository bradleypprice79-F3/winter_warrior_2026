The Obstacles to Acceleration

We’ve all seen it. A guy is really accelerating in life. And then, some time later, you see him again and he is just floating around sitting in his boat, oars just floating. What happened???

Don’t just look at him and act judgmental. Ask yourself: What could derail my acceleration?

1. Drift

A man who is not purposeful inevitably drifts. Drift is the natural state of a man with no direction.

Causes of Drift:

  • Not taking the Daily Red Pill. You have to show up!
  • Losing continuity (no disciplines or routines)
  • Lack of accountability
“Man left to his own devices will drift. Purpose and accountability arrest the Drift.”

2. Isolation

“Man is made to be in community.” Isolation is one of the fastest ways to stall out.

Isolation kills acceleration because:

  • You cannot Live Right or Lead Right alone
  • Without a Shield Lock, you are fragile under stress
  • Blind spots grow
  • Isolation increases fear, shame, and paralysis

Recall that in the first week we heard from @ScubaSteve,“Life has a way of isolating us.” Be on guard against that Isolation!

3. Comfort

There is nothing wrong with comfort. But men will stall when they choose comfort over commitment.

The Fartsack represents:

  • Laziness
  • Excuses
  • The gravitational pull toward ease
  • Avoiding discomfort and challenge
“The Comfort Zone is a cage disguised as a recliner.”

Know what your commitments are. Make them public to those who can hold you accountable. And choose commitment over comfort.

4. Fear

Fear is a universal inhibitor

  • fear of failure, rejection, responsibility

Fear + comfort = the perfect recipe for stalling out.

The opposite of fear is Courage.
“Courage is not the absence of fear, but being afraid and moving anyway.”

Additionally, the unknown is always scarier than the actual event. Choose to be courageous. Move forward into the water, even though you are afraid, and you will find that it’s never as bad as you thought it would be.

5. Shame & Isolation Loops

The shame loop:

  • A man struggles
  • He hides it
  • He isolates
  • The problem grows
  • He’s less likely to reach out
  • He quits accelerating

Community, such as a Shield Lock and a whetstone, is the antidote. Be vulnerable, communicate, and demand Candor (the telling of hard truths) from those around you.

Failure is part of the acceleration!

You were injured, you got new hours at work, things got busy, you had to travel, and so you were unable to come out for a while. And it was easier just to stay home, even when you could have come back out. FAIL!

You had quit your bad habit, but now you have found yourself back in it, yet again. FAIL!

You applied for the new job, even had some great interviews, but they went a different direction. FAIL!

No worries, Failure is part of the process. Do not allow Isolation and Shame to take root!

6. The Crack Pipe of the Status Quo

Leadership requires disrupting what is. The Status Quo resists change.

A man stalls when he avoids conflict or disruption.

“The HIM disrupts what IS to accelerate what COULD BE.”

Routine can be a good thing, and we naturally resist change because we are afraid of the unknown.

The Status Quo only thinks about WHAT IS and refuses to think about WHAT’S NEXT!?
